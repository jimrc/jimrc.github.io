Bootstrap Demo

What is a Bootstrap Resample?

Start with a known population of 100 counts of the number of customers checking out per hour.
Click "Sample" and a sample of size eight is drawn from the population, and appears at the bottom of the plot.
The population then disappears, because we don't usually know it.
Bootstrap resamples are based on just the one sample.

Click "1 Resample". You'll see a sample of size 8 get selected from the original sample with replacement. Some of the items in the sample get chosen multiple times (giving a darker background), and some not at all (background stays white). The mean of the resample is shown as a point in the plot. Click "slower" or "faster" to vary the speed.

To build a confidence interval, we need many samples, so click one of the "Many Resamples" options. Only the resample means are shown. A 95% confidence interval appears on the plot as red boundary lines. Is is computed by placing the lower bar just above 2.5% of the resamples and the upper bar just below 2.5% of the resamples. That leaves 95% of the resampled means between the two red lines.
